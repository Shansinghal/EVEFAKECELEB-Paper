Harnessing microsecond-scale neuromorphic event streams alongside standard video to build next-generation deepfake detectors that see what others miss.

🔍 About the Research

EveFakeCeleb is a pioneering dataset engineered to advance the frontier of deepfake detection. Unlike traditional datasets that rely exclusively on video frames, EveFakeCeleb uniquely combines standard RGB video data with event-based data that captures microsecond-level changes in motion — including blinks, lip movements, and micro-expressions.

These subtle physiological cues are notoriously difficult for generative models to replicate faithfully. Deepfakes often exhibit temporally inconsistent micro-expressions that, while invisible to the naked eye, leave distinct signatures in the asynchronous, high-temporal-resolution event stream. This makes event data an exceptionally powerful modality for identifying synthetic content.

By pairing RGB and event streams into a cohesive, research-ready dataset, EveFakeCeleb empowers researchers to develop more accurate, generalizable, and robust detection models that perform effectively in real-world scenarios — even against high-quality or adversarially crafted deepfakes that defeat conventional frame-based analysis.
